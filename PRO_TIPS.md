# Professional Travel Planning — Tips, Entry Rules, Safety

## What pros do that DIY travelers miss

**Booking sequence and lead times.**
- International flights: 4–7 months out (sweet spot ~5 months for transatlantic summer). You're 2 months out — fares may be elevated.
- Hotels next, once flights are locked.
- Bucket-list activities (Alhambra, San Fermín balconies) the moment they release. Alhambra opens 90 days prior. San Fermín balconies typically sold by January for July.
- Michelin/marquee restaurants 6–8 weeks ahead.
- Ground transfers last (1–2 weeks before).

**Order: Flights → Lodging → Bucket-list activities → Restaurants → Transfers → Day-of logistics.**

**Buffer time.** International connections in Europe = 2 hours minimum. Same-airline same-ticket connections only — never self-transfer with checked bags. On the ground, build a buffer day after long-haul arrival before any non-refundable activity. (Your trip has zero buffer days, so any flight delay cascades.)

**Single PNR for the same-day-to-USA day.** GRX-MAD-CLT as one Iberia/AA codeshare ticket. If Iberia is late out of Granada, AA rebooks you free; if you booked separately, you're on your own.

## Travel insurance

**Worth it on any trip with >$2K in non-refundable prepayments or international medical exposure.** Both your $7K and $9K versions qualify.

**Buy within 14–21 days of first deposit** to qualify for pre-existing condition waivers and Cancel For Any Reason (CFAR) coverage.

**What to look for:**
- ≥$100K emergency medical (primary, not secondary)
- ≥$250K medical evacuation
- 100% trip cancellation, 150% trip interruption
- Baggage delay ($200+/day)
- 24/7 assistance hotline

**Top picks:** IMG iTravelInsured, Allianz, Travel Guard, World Nomads. Cost ~5–10% of trip.

## Schengen / EU entry — 2026 status

- **EES (Entry/Exit System) is fully operational as of April 10, 2026.** Expect biometric fingerprinting and a facial scan on first Schengen entry (CDG for you). Adds 5–15 min at peak.
- **ETIAS is NOT yet active.** Rollout pushed to late 2026 / early 2027. You enter on US passport only for July 2026.
- **Passport requirement:** valid 3+ months past departure date (so through October 12, 2026 minimum), issued within last 10 years.

## Emergency contingencies

- **Photograph passport ID page** + store in cloud (Google Drive, iCloud).
- **Carry a paper copy** separate from your passport.
- **Nearest US Consulate:** Madrid (Calle Serrano 75). Consular agency in Málaga for southern Spain.
- **Lost passport:** report to local police → get a denuncia → emergency passport at consulate (1–3 days).
- **Enroll in [STEP](https://step.state.gov)** for State Dept alerts.
- **Save EU emergency number: 112.**
- **Backup card strategy:** carry one credit + one debit card on you; second of each in the hotel safe. Use different networks (Visa + Mastercard).

## Currency / payment

- **Spain and France are heavily card-friendly** — Visa/Mastercard contactless works almost everywhere, including small tapas bars.
- Bring a **no-foreign-transaction-fee card**: Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture, Charles Schwab debit.
- **Always pay in euros, not dollars** when given the choice. Dynamic Currency Conversion is a 3–7% rip-off.
- Carry **€100–200 cash** for tips, small markets, taxis in rural areas, and bathroom attendants.
- ATMs: prefer bank-branded ATMs (BBVA, Santander, CaixaBank) over Euronet/Travelex which charge inflated fees.

## San Fermín-specific safety (July 7)

- **Balcony check-in 6:30–6:45 AM** sharp. Route access closes ~7:00 AM.
- **Wear traditional white + red faja/pañuelo** to blend in.
- **Pickpocket risk during fiesta is EXTREME.** Use a money belt or front pocket only. Leave passport in hotel safe; carry a photocopy.
- **Never put a phone in a back pocket.**
- **Avoid the bull run route on foot after 6 AM** — crowds + drunks + tight streets.
- **No heavy drinking before the balcony** — rentals will refuse intoxicated guests.
- **Best balcony angles:** Calle Estafeta (longest view) or Mercaderes/Estafeta corner ("Dead Man's Curve" — iconic angle, premium price).
- **After the run (~9:30 AM):** streets are filthy with debris and broken bottles. Watch your footing. The party continues all day; pace your alcohol.

## Pro extras

1. **Master itinerary doc** with confirmation numbers, addresses, phone numbers. Email yourself everything with consistent subject tags ("SPAIN-LODGING-MAR16", etc.) for searchability.
2. **Offline Google Maps** for every city.
3. **Pre-book restaurants for Sunday/Monday** — many close.
4. **Spain dines late:** lunch 14:00–16:00, dinner 21:00–23:00. Reservations before 21:00 mark you as a tourist; book 21:30 to fit the local rhythm.
5. **Pack a small daypack** + TSA-approved padlock.
6. **Verify your bank's international ATM partner network** to avoid fees (BofA + BNP Paribas/Deutsche Bank, etc.).
7. **Strikes (huelgas)** are common in Spanish/French transport in summer — always have a Plan B for the day of departure (extra cash, refundable backup transit option).
8. **EU plug adapter** (Type C/F). Hotels usually have one but apartments rarely do.
9. **Download a translation app** (Google Translate with offline Spanish + French packs).
10. **Tip culture:** not expected at the level US tipping is. Round up the bill or leave 5–10% for sit-down restaurants. Don't tip at pintxo bars.

## Specific risks for THIS itinerary

- **July 9 BIO → GRX is a same-day connection through Madrid.** If the Iberia flight from Bilbao is delayed (common in summer), you may misconnect. Single PNR protects you.
- **July 12 GRX → MAD → CLT is the riskiest day** of the trip. AA748 departs MAD ~12:00. The 06:30 Iberia from Granada lands MAD T4 at 07:40 — you have 4h 20m at Madrid Barajas, which is enough but feels tight after the Iberia leg if there are weather delays. **Consider sleeping in Madrid the night of July 11** instead of Granada — kills one Granada night but eliminates the same-day risk. Cost: ~$150 hotel + AVE train repositioning.
- **San Fermín = packed trains, packed buses, packed everything in northern Spain.** Buy bus tickets in advance, not at the counter.
- **Heat:** Granada in July routinely hits 38–40°C (100–104°F). Sightseeing 8–11 AM and after 19:00; siesta in between like a local.
