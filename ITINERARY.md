# Spain Trip — Detailed Itinerary

**Travelers:** 2 adults (flights, Paris, Granada, all transfers). Third adult (friend) joins in San Sebastián July 6 evening, departs after Pamplona day on July 7. He shares the San Sebastián apartment and the Pamplona-day arrangements.

**Dates:** July 3–12, 2026

**Luggage assumption (every flight):** 2 checked bags + 2 carry-ons per traveler. All baggage fees included in totals below.

> All prices are estimates as of May 2026 (~2 months out from travel). Live booking sites blocked direct fetch, so flight prices are derived from route metadata + historical July transatlantic averages — treat as ±15% and confirm at booking. Lodging quotes pulled from listing pages on Booking.com/Airbnb/VRBO.

## Critical context

1. **San Fermín overlap.** July 6–9 in San Sebastián overlaps with San Fermín in Pamplona (50 mi away). Hotel rates are 40–80% above normal, and 2-bedroom apartments meeting your criteria are selling out 6+ months ahead. Many vacation rentals impose 5–7 night minimums during this week — confirm 3-night acceptance via host message before paying.
2. **July 7 is the first encierro** — the highest-demand day of the entire festival. Balconies routinely sell out by January. **Book within 1–2 weeks** or accept what's left.
3. **BIO → GRX on July 9 (Thursday) has no direct flight.** Vueling's nonstop flies Tue/Sat only. You must connect via Madrid (Iberia, ~5h30 door-to-door) or shift the date — and the date is fixed by your Granada lodging.
4. **Granada → Madrid → Charlotte same-day on July 12 is tight.** AA748 MAD-CLT departs ~12:00. You need to be at Madrid Barajas T4S by 10:00, which means the 06:30 Iberia flight from Granada (lands MAD T4 07:40) — NOT the AVE train (lands Atocha 10:45, requires a separate 45-min transit to Barajas).
5. **The friend's share.** I've assumed the friend covers his own 1/3 of the San Sebastián apartment and 1/3 of the Pamplona day. If you're covering him, add ~$700–$1,100 to your totals.

#### Tapas crawl — Calle Navas

Granada is famous for **free tapas with every drink**. Walk Calle Navas south from Plaza del Carmen — quality improves the farther you go. Order a caña or vermut at each (~€2.50); free tapa arrives. Walk-in only.

1. **Bar Los Diamantes** (Calle Navas 28) — Andalusian fried seafood since 1942
2. **Bodegas Castañeda** (Calle Almireceros 1-3, just off Navas) — wine barrels, hand-cut jamón, oxtail stew
3. **La Sitarilla** or **Bar Avila** — farther down Navas, smaller and more local

Budget ~€20–25 pp for 4 drinks + free tapas + one shared ración.
